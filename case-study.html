01 — The Problem

Scattered data, slow decisions

In most growing businesses, the numbers that matter live in separate places. Revenue sits in the payment processor, pipeline lives in the CRM, ad spend is split across platforms, and someone spends hours every week stitching it together by hand into a report that is already out of date by the time it is read.

The goal of this build was to remove that manual layer entirely: one dashboard, always current, that answers the questions leadership actually asks — how much are we making, what is it costing us to acquire customers, where is revenue coming from, and what does the pipeline look like heading into next month.

02 — The Build

Executive overview

Executive view — headline metrics, MRR trend, channel mix, and pipeline health on a single screen.

The dashboard pulls from three data sources on a scheduled refresh, so nobody has to touch it manually. Headline metrics sit at the top for the thirty-second read, with deeper breakdowns below for anyone who wants to dig in. Every number traces back to a validated source, so the reporting can be trusted for real decisions.

03 — Approach

How it was built

Data Consolidation

Connected CRM, advertising platforms, and payment data into a single reporting layer, with cleaning and validation rules applied at ingestion so errors never reach the dashboard.

Automation

Scheduled, self-refreshing data sources replace manual exports. The report is always current, eliminating the weekly hours that used to go into building it by hand.

Metric Design

Built around the metrics that drive decisions — MRR, CAC, LTV:CAC, churn, channel ROI, and weighted pipeline — rather than vanity numbers that look good but say little.

Dual Audience

Designed for both technical teams who need the detail and executives who need the headline. Layered so each audience finds what they need at the right depth.
